# Avery Industries LLC Launch Blueprint

Date created: 2026-06-16

Purpose: define the cheapest practical way to make the Avery brand stack feel like one real company.

## Core Idea

Build one shared platform for all divisions instead of separate websites for every brand.

This keeps the operating cost low while still making the company feel organized, real, and scalable.

## Mother Page

`averyindustries.com` is the parent company hub.

It should do four things:

1. Present Avery Industries LLC as the umbrella company.
2. Route visitors to the active divisions.
3. Explain what each division does.
4. Reuse the same design system, analytics, and content pipeline.

## First Three Launch Sites

1. Avery Advisory
2. AveryTech
3. Atlas Protocol

These three create the first useful triangle:

- revenue and consulting
- technology and tools
- portfolio and proof

## Shared Stack

- One frontend codebase
- One hosting target
- One CMS or content source
- One analytics layer
- One form pipeline
- One email system
- One design system with theme tokens

## Division Map

- Avery Advisory: consulting and automation revenue
- AveryTech: technology products and accessibility-friendly tools
- Atlas Protocol: portfolio, proof, and company credibility
- Creator Logistics: creator products and fulfillment
- Avery Supply Co.: apparel
- Avery Athletics: fitness and supplements
- ColeTrain Studios: art and animation production
- Avery Entertainment: media company
- Avery Foundation: charity work
- Avery Collectables: toys and collectibles
- Avery Music Group: music

## Cheapest Build Order

1. Parent company page
2. Shared brand registry
3. Division landing page template
4. Domain routing rules
5. Contact and lead capture pipeline
6. Analytics and logging
7. Add the remaining divisions as content branches

## Validation Checklist

- The parent company is always Avery Industries LLC.
- Atlas Protocol is spelled correctly everywhere.
- The launch order is revenue first.
- The stack is shared instead of duplicated.
- Every division has a domain, a purpose, and a launch stage.
- The system can grow without rebuilding the foundation.

## Notes

This blueprint is intentionally conservative. It is designed to make the brand network feel established without burning money on duplicate infrastructure.
