# Avery Industries 24-Month Realistic Timeline

Date created: 2026-06-04

Purpose: realistic execution timeline for Avery Industries, ATLAS Command Center, ATLAS Assist, smart-glasses direction, disability-aid workflows, and company growth.

This timeline assumes Avery Industries focuses on revenue, ATLAS Core, Creator Logistics, and accessibility evidence before trying to build every long-term idea.

## Months 15-18: Facial and Hand-Signal Research Layer

Goal: research interaction methods safely.

Facial learning:

- Treat as a high-risk feature.
- Start with voluntary user-only face state calibration, not identifying other people.
- Avoid public facial recognition.
- Use local processing where possible.
- Require explicit consent and privacy controls.

Hand-signal learning:

- Start with simple intentional gestures:
  - open hand
  - thumbs up
  - point
  - pinch
  - stop
  - tap sequence
- Use gestures as command shortcuts.
- Avoid interpreting accidental movement as commands.

Safety requirements:

- Clear opt-in.
- Local-first processing preferred.
- No covert recording.
- No identification of bystanders.
- No medical claims.
- Manual override.

Expected outcome:

- ATLAS has a research-backed interaction plan for gesture and facial-state support.

## 24-Month Outcome

Realistic best case after 24 months:

- Creator Logistics produces revenue.
- ATLAS Command Center runs the company.
- ATLAS Assist is a usable phone-based assistant.
- AveryTech has pilot evidence.
- Smart-glasses prototype exists.
- Facial/hand-signal learning is in safe research prototype stage.
- ATLAS can support reminders, active memory recall, location tags, routines, and task simplification.

What is not realistic in 24 months without major funding:

- Fully autonomous JARVIS.
- Perfect always-on memory.
- Production-grade facial recognition.
- Full custom smart-glasses hardware.
- Medical-grade disability aid certification.
- Replacing human caregivers, therapists, or doctors.

## Permanent Safety Rules

- Human approval before risky action.
- Camera and microphone features must be opt-in.
- No covert recording.
- No bystander identification.
- No unsupported medical claims.
- No external sharing of sensitive life data without approval.
- Local-first processing should be preferred for disability-aid and wearable contexts.
